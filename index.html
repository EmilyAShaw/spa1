Task

The task, for which I completed was to go around Wellington city and discover the signs of internet infrastructure in this city. I took photos of what I found (some photo credit is given to Zoe Richards as my phone died so I lost about two weeks worth of photos and had no backup). I did do illustrator drawings of some of the photographs as felt it drew attention to the parts of the images which are the infrastructure - in case someone like me who started off with no idea about this, came across my website.

Findings

I found, through my experience of walking through not only the center of town, but places like Newtown and Haitaitai, that there is internet infrastructure everywhere. I actually found it easier to identify and find infrastructure in smaller townships like those, rather than around places you would think they be (such as kelburn campus). I think this is probably due the signs being hidden more in university, and me not being able to find them - rather than Haitaitai having more infrastructure.

Buildings

Datacom

This is the datacom building in lambton quay, they are a wellington central hosting service for bussiness and more. They have Huge servers to do so, and they are an important part of wellington internet infrastructure. They also have two other locations in wellington, like abel smith street and one other.

Spark Building

The spark buolding on willis street is home to many internet based services, such as the 111 call center, and as well as Datacom also houses servers. This makes it another important part of Wellington infrastrucure.

Transforemer

This is not really a building, however it was relativly big. This is the transformer outside kelburn University. This supports the univerity's power and therfore internet. It is one of many transformers in the courtyard.

Example

I did not take this photo, but I feel this was an important park of infrastructure to include, as it is the inter island link, which connects the north island and south island internet. This cable is commonly refered to as the 'Cook Strait cable' and is ownded by Transpower New Zealand. "The link starts at the Benmore Hydroelectric Power Station, on the Waitaki River in Canterbury, and travels 534 kilometres (332 mi) on an overhead transmission line through inland Canterbury and Marlborough to Fighting Bay in the Marlborough Sounds. From Fighting Bay, the link travels 40 km via submarine cables across Cook Strait to Oteranga Bay, near Wellington, before travelling the final 37 km on overhead lines to Haywards transmission substation in Lower Hutt." - Wikipedia
